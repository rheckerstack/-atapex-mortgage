01 — Eligibility

Resident vs Non-Resident

The rules are fundamentally different. Know which category your client falls into before you start — it saves weeks.

🇦🇪

UAE Residents — Local Income

Valid UAE visa + Emirates ID · income in UAE

20% down payment mandatory minimum
Min salary AED 10,000/mo — more banks open at AED 15,000+
Min 6 months with current employer (options after 1 month)
Self-employed: min 1 year trading (LTV depends on business profile + bank)
Age at maturity: up to 65 (salaried) / 70 (self-employed)
Up to 80% LTV on ready properties ≤ AED 5M
Every UAE liability counts — credit cards, car loans, personal loans all hit your DBR. Keep them low.
Personal loan in last 3–6 months may cause decline or affect bank decision
Income earned abroad = treated as non-resident regardless of UAE visa status
🌎

Non-Residents & Income Abroad

International buyers · income earned abroad

40% down payment mandatory minimum
Max LTV: 50–60% depending on client profile and bank
Salaried: salary certificate + 6 months payslips
Self-employed: accounts + 2 years tax returns
2 years tax returns required in all cases
Company registration documents if applicable
Home country credit bureau report (bank-dependent)
6 months personal bank statements
Better approval odds at handover / ready property vs off-plan
Not all banks handle non-residents — AtApex identifies the right fit per profile

04 — Live Calculator

Run your numbers

Edit any field — EMI, DBR, DSCR, investment returns all update live. Age automatically caps your loan term.

DBR — Debt Burden Ratio
Total monthly debt ÷ gross income. UAE cap: 50%. Banks prefer 45% or below. Every liability counts — car loans, credit cards, personal loans.
(EMI + all debts) ÷ income × 100
DTI — Debt-to-Income
Same as DBR — used interchangeably. International buyers know this term. Same 50% cap. Aim for 43% or below for maximum bank options.
total monthly debts ÷ gross income
DSCR — Debt Service Coverage
Does rental income alone cover the mortgage? Used by private funds. DSCR > 1.0 = property pays for itself. > 1.25 = excellent.
monthly rent ÷ monthly EMI

05 — Off-Plan

How off-plan financing works

The bank bridges your later developer payments — but only after specific conditions are met. Your cash always goes in first.

1
You pay 50% to developer
Your cash first — out of pocket. Non-negotiable for residents before the bank will engage.
2
35% construction completed
Bank requires this milestone before confirming bridge financing. Varies by bank and developer.
3
Pre-approval confirmed
Bank agrees to bridge remaining developer payments. Start this early — don’t wait for handover.
4
Bank pays developer directly
At each milestone the bank releases funds directly to the developer on your behalf.
5
Handover — full mortgage
Seamless transition to principal + interest. No new application. Up to 25 year term.

06 — Self-Employed

Net retention & bank statements

For self-employed clients, banks don’t just look at income — they analyse business health through 6–12 months of statements. Net retention is the most important metric most brokers don’t explain properly.

What is Net Retention?
Net retention is the percentage of total business inflows that stays after all outflows. Banks use it to prove the business is profitable — not just turning over revenue.

If AED 150k comes in and AED 120k goes out, retention is 20% — too low. If AED 50k stays, that’s 33% — solid. The retained amount builds your average daily balance (ADB) and proves real owner benefit.
Monthly inflows: AED 150k–200k
Outflows (ideal 60–70%): AED 90k–130k
Retained monthly: AED 50k+ (30–40%+)
Min ADB: 3× monthly EMI
💡
Broker tip: Ask the bank manager directly — “What net retention do you consider strong for a 50k income profile?” Emirates NBD, ADCB, and ADIB all assess this differently. One benchmark does not fit all.
Banks also cross-check
2 years audited financials — net profit, not just turnover
Sector-specific profit margins (15–30% depending on industry)
AECB credit report — no defaults, no high utilisation
Liquidity reserves beyond the down payment
Average Daily Balance (ADB) ≥ 3× monthly EMI
